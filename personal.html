What I Believe In

I have spent a lot of time learning and exploring the internet, and these are my personal opinions on it. I believe that software, in general, should be free—not just in terms of cost, but in terms of transparency. Software should be open and accessible, allowing anyone to view its inner workings, verify its integrity, and ensure its security. People should have the ability to understand where their data is going, how it’s being used, and who has access to it.

I understand that there are valid reasons for closed-source software, such as preventing cheating in games or protecting proprietary algorithms. However, I believe that, in most cases, transparency should be prioritized. The internet is a vast and powerful tool, and when used responsibly, it can enable incredible innovation and collaboration. I hope to see a future where software is more open, trustworthy, and aligned with the values of freedom and transparency.
